Tips for Visiting London

  • Skip the car rental: Parking is limited and the TFL network is more reliable. Most stations have a train or bus every 2–15 minutes.
  • Use tap-to-pay cards: No need to purchase an Oyster card; any contactless debit or credit card will work.
  • Pack light: Many tube stations lack elevators. Be ready for stairs, wide gaps between train and platform, and tight transfers.
  • Take the Thames River Cruise: From Greenwich to the city center, it’s a scenic and peaceful alternative to the Tube.
  • Follow escalator etiquette: Walk on the left, stand on the right. Blocking the left side is a major faux pas!
  • Expect loud Tube noise: Bring earphones or noise-reducing headphones. Dead zones are common—consider packing a book.
  • Enjoy free museums: Many top museums (British Museum, National Gallery) are completely free to enter.
  • Splurge on a West End show: London’s theater scene is world-class and absolutely worth it.
  • Don’t rely on Discover cards: Stick to Visa or Mastercard for best acceptance across the UK.
  • Use Google Maps public transport mode: It’s the easiest way to figure out Tube, bus, and walking routes.
  • No tipping necessary: Most restaurants add a 10–13% service fee, and tipping beyond that is not expected. Ask to remove the charge if desired.
  • Plan by neighborhood: Use Rick Steves' free audio walking tours to guide your itinerary.
